Yom HaShoah V'hag'vurah

General Notes

The 27th of Nisan was designated by the Israeli K'nesset as Yom HaShoah, a day to remember those who perished in the Holocaust and to honor those who fought in resistance movements. There is no fixed liturgy for this day, but most ceremonies do include El Malei Rachamim for the victims of the Shoah, as well as a communal Mourner's Kaddish. Mishkan T'filah includes a service for Yom HaShoah (MT 521), and the Plaut commentary suggests Torah and Haftarah readings.

Shacharit Torah Reading 

Deuteronomy 4:30-40 (P 1342, RP 1601, WTC 1069)

Shacharit Haftarah 

2 Samuel 1:17-27 (P 1667, RP 1472)
