Shabbat Zachor

General Notes

Shabbat Zachor is the second in a series of five special Shabbatot before Pesach. On Shabbat Zachor, we commemorate the actions of Amalek, who attacked the Israelites after they left Egypt. The Historic Haftarah from I Samuel continues on this theme, recalling a battle between the Israelites and Amalekites during the days of King Saul. In this Haftarah, the king of the Amalekites is identified as Agag. Shabbat Zachor occurs the week before Purim because, in M'gilat Esther, Haman is called an Agagite, and is thus considered to be a descendant of Amalek. Some Reform synagogues read a selection from the book of Esther as an alternative to the Historic Haftarah.
