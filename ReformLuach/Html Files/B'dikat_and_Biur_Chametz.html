B'dikat and Biur Chameitz

General Notes

The evening before Pesach (or the Thursday night before Pesach when Pesach begins on Saturday night), we search for chameitz in our homes as soon as possible after dark. We place pieces of chameitz in different areas of our house, in order to ensure that there is some chameitz to find during our search. We search darkened rooms by the light of a flashlight or candle. The procedure is as follows:

1. Recite the blessing before searching for chameitz, which concludes with "al biur chameitz." (The full blessing can be found at the beginning of most Haggadot.)

2. Collect the pieces of chameitz you set aside, as well as any other chameitz you find in the process of searching.

3. Recite the formula by which you renounce ownership over any chameitz you have inadvertently left in your house. (The formula begins with "Kol chamira..." and can be found at the beginning of most Haggadot.)

4. Set aside the chameitz you found during your search until the morning.

5. The following morning, before the time at which it becomes forbidden to eat chameitz, we destroy the chameitz that remains in our possession. Many destroy their chameitz by burning it, though it is permissible to destroy or dispose of it in other ways (as long as it does not remain in your home). After destroying the chameitz, we recite another passage renouncing ownership over any chameitz that we have inadvertently kept in our homes (The formula begins "kol chamira" and can be found at the beginning of most Haggadot).  When Pesach begins on Saturday night, Biur Chameitz is done on Friday without reciting this formula.

*When Pesach begins on Saturday night, make sure to leave enough chameitz for Shabbat.
